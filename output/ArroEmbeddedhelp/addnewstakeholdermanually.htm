Add new stakeholder manually

Use this screen to add the details for a new stakeholder or a handful of new stakeholders.

StakeholdersA person or organisation who has an interest in your projects. are becoming more important to schools as schools become more versatile, working in different ways with different groups.

You may have stakeholder data in numerous places e.g. in ParentPay or Excel spreadsheets on the school business manager’s computer. With Arro you can bring all your stakeholder data together in one place and then run campaigns for your projects based on selecting the right stakeholder groups for the campaigns from within Arro. If you have a lot of data to bring into Arro, we recommend that you import stakeholder data from a CSV file. But if you have a handful of stakeholders you want to add details for, it is as quick to enter these manually as it would be to set up an import file for them.

How do I ...

Check someone is eligible for Gift Aid?
  • Check the requirements for eligibility on the HMRC website by clicking here.
Download the Gift Aid declaration form?
  • Click Template alongside This contact is eligible for Gift Aid.
Add stakeholder details?
  1. Select the stakeholder profile type from the drop-down list.
    2. What profile type are there?

    Stakeholder profiles can be:

    • Pupil
    • Family
    • Alumni
    • Staff
    • Person
    • Business
    • Community Organisation
    • Charity
    • Person
    • Organisation
    • User

    The profile type gives an indication of whether they are eligible for Gift Aid or to be a volunteer for your school. For example, a pupil will not be eligible for Gift Aid, as Gift Aid donors are UK taxpayers. However you may link to the Pupil for your records but enter the parent’s name as the Gift Aid donor.

    An alumni or person profile type may or may not be eligible for Gift Aid. This depends on whether or not they are retired and if they are, whether they still pay tax. For example, if a retired person has savings and pays Income or Capital Gains Tax of an equal or higher amount than that being claimed (25p in the pound), then they are eligible for Gift Aid.

  2. Enter the stakeholder name and address details.
  3. Click Add Contact Details and enter the contact details for the stakeholder and their preferred method of contact.
  4. If the stakeholder is eligible for Gift Aid or wants to volunteer, click Add Gift Aid/Volunteer Status.
    • If a Gift Aid donor, select the check box This contact is eligible for Gift Aid and enter the Gift Aid donor name. This is the name of the person who is responsible for the gift aid (e.g. the tax payer).

      • Note: The donor’s name is a legal requirement so if you don’t record the name here then donations aren’t recorded with Gift Aid.

    • If you need to download a PDF Gift Aid Declaration form, click Template.
    • If a volunteer, select the Volunteer Status check box.
  5. If the stakeholder has a Twitter account, click Add Twitter account and add the stakeholder’s Twitter username into the Twitter box.
  6. Click Create.
