Grants Dashboard

Grants can be a source of income for your school projects. If you are successful in your grant application this can take you quite a way towards meeting the project target. So use this screen to search for and find grants which could potentially help your different school projects. You can attach the grants to your project once you have identified the ones you want to apply for.

Some words of caution!

  • Many schools may apply for the same grant and so you may not be successful however worthy your project.
  • If you are awarded a grant, there may be limitations on how you can use it.
  • Grants are finite, monetary grants tend to be one-off injections of capital so you will need other sources of income for your projects as well.

Grants are updated weekly with those expired disappearing from the list and new ones being added, so keep searching weekly as you may find something new to apply to.

Identify the different types of grant?
Type Icon Description

Award

An award provides you with support in the form of money and/or other support in achieving your goals.

Discount

A discount enables you to buy goods and services cheaper, or obtain cash back for the school when the discount is applied.

Competition

A competition is a way of providing resources to your school for the best entrant in a particular endeavour.

Fundraising

There are schemes you can take part in which assist you in fundraising.

Cash

Cash grants are available in particular areas of interest.

Support

There are government funded or various organisational schemes for promoting subjects and enhancing the employability of young people.

Freebie

Various organisations provide free resources which you can use to supplement teaching in school.
Identify how easy or difficult it is to apply for a grant?

The amount of detail that you will require to complete an application for the grant is defined in the Detail Required column.

Basic Requires the least detail.
Moderate Requires a moderate amount of detail.
Complex Requires the most detail.
Identify how long it will take to apply for a grant?

The amount of time the grant application takes to complete is shown in the To Complete column. When you need less information to complete the grant application, it takes less time to complete, so

Detail Required Time To Complete
Basic It takes only a few hours to apply.
Moderate It takes only a few days to apply.
Complex It takes a few days to apply.
Identify if a grant has a deadline?

If the grant has a deadline this is shown in the Deadline column.

Search for grants?
  • Click Search to open a free text Search box where you can enter and search on text.

  • Use the filters at the top of the screen to shorten the list of grants to choose from. You can filter the list by Project Type (e.g. to find grants for languages or for physical education), by Grant Type (e.g. if the grant is by award, through a competition, etc), by Difficulty (the amount of detail required and the amount of time it will take to complete your application) or by Status (e.g. new, updated or expiring grants).

Show new grants?
  • Select New from the Status drop-down.
Read grant details?
  • Click the Grant Title to display the grant details.
Check the eligibility of the grant?
  1. Click the Grant Title to display the grant details.
  2. Click Read More to see a full description of the grant details.
  3. Read the Who is eligible? section within the description.
Mark grants as favourites?

From the grant list:

  • Click the star icon next to the left of the Grant Title.

    • Note: If the star is yellow, it has already been marked as a favourite.

From the grant details:

  • Click Read More.
  • Click Add to Starred.
Show favourite grants?
  • Click the grey star on the filter bar at the top of the screen. This will display the list of grants that have been marked as favourites.

    • Note: To access all grants again, click the yellow star on the filter bar at the top of the screen.

Attach grants to a project?

This is not the same as applying for the grant; this process attaches the grant to the Grants tab within the project.

  1. Click the Grant Title to display the grant details.
  2. Click Add to Project.
  3. Select which projects you want to add the grant to.

Note: Grants can also be attached to projects from the project Grants tab.

Apply for a grant?

You apply for a grant through the funderâ€™s guidelines on their own website. You can access the link to funderâ€™s website and instructions on how to apply for a grant from within Grants or Projects, if you have attached the grant to a project.

Note: We recommend accessing the information from within the project as this sets the context of the grant application for you. However, if you are applying for a grant that will be used for several projects, the application must take account of the aims of all projects the grant is for.

  1. Locate the grant you want to apply for and click the Grant Title to display an outline of information about the grant.
  2. Click Read More.
  3. Follow the information provided in the What Next section of the grant information.
